Learn On Your Own

We have conducted our own research to evaluate how important of an issue vaccination exemptions are in New York City. If you want to learn more about this topic, you can check the resources we've provided below.

#1. Lessons on Vaccines

The Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia started a project called “The Vaccine Maker Project”. This project helps schools educate students, from elementary to highschool, about vaccines. They have planned lessons for teachers, videos/animations, and a website that has resources and further information about vaccines.

Click here to learn more.

#2. American Red Cross

The American Red Cross is a nonprofit organization that responds to emergencies, natural disasters, and educate America to be prepared for emergencies. Using keywords like "vaccine" and "vaccinations" will bring you to a list of search results.

Click here to learn more. Don't forget to donate to the Red Cross.


#3. The New York Times

The New York Times is a newspaper that has been covering the news for 168 years since its founding in 1851. The American newspaper has been awarded the most Pulitzer Prizes, 127, which is given for “excellence in newspaper journalism”. Multiple reports state that the New York Times is highly factual for utilizing proper sources and having journalists & editors with respectable credentials.

  • How Far Would You Go to Avoid Vaccinating Your Child? by Ginia Bellafante
  • Measles Outbreak: What Are Religious Exemptions for Vaccinations? by Elizabeth Dias
  • Get Vaccinated or Leave School: 26,000 N.Y. Children Face a Choice by Sharon Otterman
  • Measles Cases Reach Highest Level in More Than 25 Years, C.D.C. Says by Liam Stack

#4. Non-Profit Organizations

The Human Vaccines Project has a mission to support the development of vaccines and immunotherapies. They hope to fight against diseases and cancers by analyzing the immune system and using artificial intelligence for healthier, longer lives.

Click here to learn more about the Human Vaccines Project.

The New York State Vaccines for Children (VFC) Program is a program that helps give children the vaccines they require. This program provides clinics with vaccines and families pay nothing for the vaccines they are given as long as they meet the VFC criteria.

Click here to learn more about the VFC Program.

The New York State Association of County Health Officials (NYSACHO) strives to support local health departments & health and prevent disease, disability, and injury in order to improve health and safety in New York State communities.

Click here to learn more about NYSACHO.


#5. Scholarly Journals

The following scholarly journals have been peer reviewed. This means that other experts who share the same or similar professions in a particular field have evaluated the journals for high-quality, reliable information.

  • Personal beliefs exemption from mandatory immunization of children for school entry by Alexander M. Capron
  • Communicating the benefits and risks of vaccines by Sanford R. Kimmel and Robert M. Wolfe
  • Edward Jenner and the history of smallpox and vaccination by Stefan Riedel

#6. Laws and Policies

Governor Cuomo signed a law that does not allow parents to exempt their children from being vaccinated for non-medical reasons.

Click here to learn more

For the 2019-2020 school year, New York State Public Health Law Section 2164 and New York Codes, Rules and Regulations (NYCRR) Title 10, Subpart 66-1 require every student entering a NYS school “to be immune to diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis, measles, mumps, rubella, poliomyelitis, hepatitis B, varicella and meningococcal” with varying dosages according to age.

Click here to learn more.

The New York City (NYC) Department of Health and Mental Hygiene has made the "City Health Information" available to the public. The NYC Health Department discusses vaccine safety, the influenza, and vaccine recommendations.

Click here to learn more.
